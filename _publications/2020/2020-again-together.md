abstract: >-
  To share a virtual reality (VR) experience remotely together, users typically record videos
  from an individual’s point of view and co-watch them later. However, video-based sharing limits
  users to the original capture perspective. We present ReliveInVR, a time-machine-like VR
  experience sharing system that allows multiple users to relive a recorded VR experience
  together while freely exploring it from any viewpoint. We conducted a 1×3 within-subject
  study with 26 dyads comparing ReliveInVR to co-watching 360-degree videos on desktop and in VR.
  Results show that ReliveInVR led to higher immersion, stronger social presence, and better
  understanding of shared experiences. Participants also reported discovering unnoticed details
  together and found the experience more fulfilling. We discuss design implications for
  asynchronous and remote sharing of immersive VR experiences.
